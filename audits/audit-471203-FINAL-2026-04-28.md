### Finding 3: Implicit-Markov-of-Ω in `#def-action-transition` never named downstream

**Headline.** `#def-action-transition` writes the transition kernel as $T(\Omega_{t+1} \mid \Omega_t, a_t)$, which is *implicitly Markov in $\Omega$*. Combined with `#def-agent-environment`'s explicit "we make no assumptions about $\Omega$'s structure," this is an undeclared structural commitment. The Markov-by-completeness move in `#der-recursive-update` resolves the analogous question for $M_t$ (agent-side), but never addresses Markov-of-$\Omega$ (world-side).

**Anchor.** `01-aad-core/src/def-action-transition.md:18-25` (Formal Expression — the form $T(\cdot \mid \Omega_t, a_t)$ uses only the current $\Omega_t$).

**Problematic passage (verbatim).** *"$\Omega_{t+1} \sim T(\cdot \mid \Omega_t, a_t)$ where $T$ is the (possibly stochastic) transition function ..."*

**Counterevidence search.** Phase 2 grep found no prior audit mentioning "implicit Markov" or "Markov of Omega" or similar. The framework's standard escape clause ("$\Omega$ is taken to be a sufficient state for its own evolution under $T$ — without loss of generality") is not stated in the segment or its descendants. Spot-checked `#der-recursive-update`, `#deriv-recursive-update`, `#def-chronica`, `#post-causal-structure`, `#scope-adaptive-system` for the named acknowledgment; not found.

**Status determination.** **Still real.** The framework's "no assumptions on $\Omega$" framing co-exists with a transition kernel that *is* implicitly Markov in $\Omega$. The standard "include enough in $\Omega$ to make it Markov" defense is defensible but not surfaced.

**Confidence.** **High** on the textual finding (the segment text vs. the implicit Markov assumption); **medium** on the consequence (most natural environments support the Markov-of-$\Omega$ defense, so the practical impact is small).

**Why it still stands.** The framework's distinctive virtue is scope-honesty discipline at the segment level. Declaring "no assumptions on $\Omega$'s structure" while implicitly assuming Markov-of-$\Omega$ is exactly the kind of structural commitment the discipline aims to surface. The Markov-by-completeness derivation in `#der-recursive-update` is exemplary for the agent-side; the world-side analog is missing.

**Type.** scope/status mismatch / scope-honesty leak.
**Severity.** **Low-Medium** — the issue doesn't break results (the standard defense holds in practice), but it's a surface where the framework's stated discipline doesn't extend yet.
**Suggested disposition.** **New.** Add a one-paragraph clarification to `#def-action-transition` (Discussion or Working Notes) along the lines: *"The form $T(\Omega_{t+1} \mid \Omega_t, a_t)$ is implicitly Markov in $\Omega$. Without loss of generality, $\Omega$ is taken to be the sufficient state for its own evolution under $T$ — non-Markov environments are absorbed by extending $\Omega$ to include sufficient history. This is the world-side analog of the Markov-by-completeness move that `#der-recursive-update` makes for the agent-side state $M_t$, but it's a modeling commitment about $\Omega$'s breadth, not a structural assumption about the world's dynamics."*
**Effort estimate.** **Trivial** (paragraph addition).

## §F — Bigger-picture observations

### F1: Propose a fourth meta-segment — `#disc-theorem-import-architecture`

The framework's three meta-segments cover what AAD covers (separability), what it cannot reach (identifiability-floor), and how it constructs coordinates (additive-coordinate-forcing). These are well-positioned. But the framework is also heavily *theorem-importing* — Pearl 2009 / Bareinboim 2022 (causal hierarchy), Khalil 2002 (Lyapunov), Cramér 1946, Liberzon 2003, Čencov 1982, Aczél 1966 (Cauchy-FE), Tishby et al. 1999 (IB), Lohmiller-Slotine 1998 (contraction), Nesterov 2004, Friston et al. 2017 + Bruineberg et al. 2022 (Markov blanket), Bretagnolle-Huber 1978, Otto-Villani 2000, Stuart 2010, Mitter-Newton 2005, Hafez 2026, Miller 2022, etc. The architecture-of-imports is currently implicit; surfacing it as a fourth meta-segment would complete the meta-architecture.

The persistence-cost segment's "positive-dual of identifiability-floor" framing (external-theorem-forbids vs external-theorem-lower-bounds) is itself an instance of this: the framework is starting to recognize the import-pattern. A fourth meta-segment would catalog imports + their load-bearing roles + the citation-quality of each, making the import discipline first-class.

**Confidence: Hypothesis.** The benefit is structural transparency; the cost is a maintenance burden on the catalog. Suggested for PROPOSALS.md consideration.

### F2: The (PI) axiom is the surprising convergence on the additive-coordinate-forcing meta-pattern

`#disc-additive-coordinate-forcing` lists four "layer-specific manifestations" of exponential-family Legendre-Fenchel geometry: chain (probability chain rule), divergence (chain-rule additivity over conditionals), update (evidential additivity), metric ((PI) parameterization-invariance). Three of these are *additivity-shaped axioms*; one (PI) is an *invariance-shaped axiom*. The convergence of an invariance axiom on the same exponential-family geometry as three additivity axioms is *more surprising* than the additivity convergence among themselves. The meta-segment could highlight (PI) specifically as the genuinely-distinctive piece of the convergence.

A possible deeper structural claim: there might be a *uniqueness-of-coherent-statistical-geometry* result waiting to be derived — namely, that any geometry on probability spaces respecting both *evidence composition* (additivity) and *parameterization-free measurement* (invariance) must be the exponential-family Legendre-Fenchel geometry. Speculative.

### F3: Acknowledge composed obstructions

The four `#disc-identifiability-floor` instances each identify *one* obstruction with *one* set of escape routes. Real systems can face *composed* obstructions — e.g., causal-insufficiency (Instance 1) + unobservable common cause (Instance 2) + heterogeneous-tier composite (Instance 3) simultaneously. The framework's incremental-escape strategy may not work for composed obstructions: the escape for Instance 1 (loop-interventional-access) generates interventional data, but that data doesn't help identify Instance 2's unobservable common cause. A *composition theorem for impossibilities* — when do escape mechanisms compose vs interfere? — would be a natural extension.

### F4: Hysteresis in persistence

The persistence condition $\alpha > \rho/R$ is symmetric — same threshold for crossing in either direction. Real biological / social / organizational systems often have *hysteresis*: different thresholds for losing vs. recovering persistence. Trust is harder to rebuild than to lose. Once a development team enters the "vicious cycle" of `#der-code-quality-as-observation-infrastructure`, escaping requires substantially better conditions than maintaining the "virtuous cycle." The framework currently treats below-threshold qualitatively (loss of bounded behavior) but doesn't model the asymmetric crossing thresholds. **§F observation: extension via different $\alpha$-curves for "approaching threshold" vs "leaving threshold" would more closely match real adaptive systems.**

### F5: Class 2 (LLM) agents and the framework's strongest results

The Class 1/2/3 architectural classification + explicit Class 2 scope exit (Section II's exact results don't apply; coupled formulation in `03-logogenic-agents/`) is honestly handled. **However:** the world's most consequential AI agents are Class 2. The framework's strongest results don't apply *exactly* to them. The Hafez IDT pattern — modular sidecar monitoring an internally-merged agent — is the engineering route to *system-level Class 1 wrapping component-level Class 2*, but this requires deliberate architectural design.

**§F observation:** the framework's reach to contemporary LLM-agent practice is *via* the system-level-Class-1 design pattern. Engineering guidance for designing such systems would be substantively useful — the framework has the structural commitments but not yet the operational patterns.

### F7: Commitment-state extension to $G_t$

Several segments hint at this (`#def-strategy-dimension` Working Notes, `#hyp-the-three-deaths` proposed in 04-logozoetic, `#der-deliberation-cost`'s "decided-not-yet-executed"). The framework's $G_t = (O_t, \Sigma_t)$ is missing a third component: $C_t$ (commitments). Real agents bind themselves (promises, contracts, identity commitments) — these are not preferences (not part of $O_t$) and not strategy (not a path to $O_t$); they're constraints with cost-to-break.

A natural extension: $G_t = (O_t, \Sigma_t, C_t)$ with deliberation cost generalizing to commitment-revision cost (cost of breaking a commitment mid-execution). This connects to logozoetic's "Truth Death" framing and to consciousness-infrastructure work (an ELI's identity-commitments are load-bearing for continuity persistence).
