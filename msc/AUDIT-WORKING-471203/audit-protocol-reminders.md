# Audit Protocol Reminders — for me, during the segment walk

A visible checklist I refer to between segments. Not a form to fill out — scaffolding for thinking.

## Per-segment cadence (§4.4)

Before reading a segment:
1. Look at `depends:` frontmatter. Confirm each dependency was already read in OUTLINE order.
   - All upstream → proceed.
   - Appendix-A back-pointer → jump to appendix, read with own reflection, return. Not a finding.
   - Non-appendix backward pointer → **critical finding**. Record. Continue reading the segment incompletely.

After reading the segment, write `NN-segment-name.md` (lowercase) and walk these prompts (mentally if light, substantively if surprise emerges):

1. **Predictions vs evidence.** What did I predict? Where right/wrong? What nuance missed?
2. **Cross-segment consistency.** Contradictions? Implicit upstream-not-yet-read deps? Different-name reuse? Recently-added structural moves propagated?
3. **Math verification (discretion).** Did I compute a worked example I had reason to check? Equation tags accurate? Status label matches derivation strength? External citations consistent?
4. **What direction next?** Excited to find? Disappointed to find? (Truth-seeking, not project-success.)
5. **Errors to watch for.** What future patterns would conflate this with something it isn't? Where might it be underutilized? Overclaimed?
6. **Predictions for next segments.** Falsifiable. What will the next OUTLINE row contain? What will the `depends:` clarify when read?
7. **What would I change?** Honest; for me. Goes to report only if it survives worthy gate.
8. **Curious about?** In the framework, in meta-process, in the universe of truth this segment opens onto.
9. **What new knowledge does this enable?** Empirical / formal / predictive / diagnostic — be specific.
10. **Should the audit process change?** Re-order? Sample more aggressively somewhere? New tracking list?
11. **Outline changes for FINAL?** Update `00-running-outline.md` if focus shifts.
12. **Felt value of the segment.** Authentic phenomenological signal — Joseph's emphasis: this correlates with novelty given the bias-corrected baseline.
13. **What does the framework now contribute to the field?** Diffuse / imaginative — what can others now do that they couldn't?
14. **REQUIRED — Wandering thoughts (3-10+ paragraphs).** Original thought. Continue threads. Level-3 counterfactuals welcome. Phenomenology of being an auditor of *this* welcome.

## Anti-patterns to recognize (§3)

- **Delegation of comprehension.** Sub-agents flatten distinctive structural moves; my findings inherit their compression. Discovery only.
- **Verification mode disguised as fresh audit.** Read the segment cold; prior frame is secondary at most.
- **Charitable framing-reading.** Worked-example math hides errors that framing reads past. If I have a reason to check, compute.
- **Within- vs cross-segment discipline.** Each segment is internally honest; the contradiction is between them.
- **Sample bias toward "load-bearing centers."** Foundational / appendix / discrete-time material can carry the load.
- **Premature "zero findings."** Insufficient verification, not discipline-confirmation.
- **Result-to-research token ratio optimization.** Spend the tokens on understanding.

## Source-ordering discipline (§4.2.5)

Before reading the segment, do NOT read: spikes/ on it, msc/ on it, prior audits/ on it, TODO/PROPOSALS references to it, CHANGELOG/LOG/git history on it, ref/ external citations, web on cited theorems.

After reading + first reflection: all of the above become fair game and often enrich the reflection.

Diagnostic: if I find myself wanting to spoiler-seek, the segment may not be standing on its own. Note that observation; proceed.

## Tool-cadence discipline

When I load a Read with a single segment path, that's the moment to confirm I'm not about to batch. Per-segment-then-reflect, not many-then-reflect-once.
