## Framework topology as I understand it

AAD has three sections with different epistemic maturity levels:
- Section I (adaptive systems): mathematically closed, Lyapunov proofs, simulation-validated. This is where the persistence condition, mismatch dynamics, and gain structure live.
- Section II (actuated agents): strong diagnostic core (satisfaction-gap / control-regret split), maturing operational layer. Strategy as probabilistic DAG with proved Markov property and acyclicity from temporal ordering.
- Section III (composition): has bridge lemma and contraction-template generalization; composition admissibility is formulated not derived.

The four components (AAD-core, TST, logogenic, logozoetic) are at very different stages. TST is the calibration laboratory where AAD's formalism meets engineering empirics. Logogenic agents are where the directed-separation scope condition fails by construction.
