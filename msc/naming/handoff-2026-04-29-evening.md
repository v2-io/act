## Pipeline architecture

```
naming-votes/*.md
   │  bin/naming-aggregate.rb
   ▼
naming-aggregate-r2-{review,compact,round2,votes.json}
   │  bin/naming-master-init
   ▼
master-list.json  ── auto-regenerated; do NOT hand-edit
   │  (initial copy → curated)
   ▼
master-list-curated.json  ── manually curated + agent-pass enriched
   │  bin/naming-master-view (defaults to curated)
   │  bin/naming-master-card --seed=<agent-id>
   ▼
master-list-{compact,full,summary}.md  +  round-2-cards/<seed>.md
```

The separate-file workflow (auto vs curated) is critical — running `bin/naming-master-init` would regenerate `master-list.json` and destroy hand-curation. The view scripts default to reading curated when present.

The naming-cycle work in this session benefited substantially from Joseph's coaching on peer-to-peer voice when writing briefs for sub-agents (memory: `feedback_peer_to_peer_voice_when_instructing_agents.md`). The Pass A and Pass B briefs (in `msc/naming/`) are worked examples of the principle — they set intent and posture, name what's at stake, and trust the receiving agent's tactical judgment rather than prescribing it.
