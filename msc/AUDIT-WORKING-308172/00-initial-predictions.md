## Topology of the framework

- AAD = the substrate. Three sections + appendices, with Section I (adaptive systems) most mathematically locked, Section II (actuated agents) diagnostic-strong with conditional theorems, Section III (composition) has structural framing with a bridge lemma still depending on contraction assumptions.
- Three meta-segments form the cross-sectional structure: `#disc-separability-pattern` (positive), `#disc-identifiability-floor` (negative no-go's), `#disc-additive-coordinate-forcing` (constructive log-additive moves). The README is explicit that reading any segment "through all three lenses surfaces what makes it load-bearing." I'd expect about a third of the load-bearing intuition voters develop to come from those three.
- Section I: cycle (5 phases, Greek), mismatch dynamics, gain, tempo, persistence condition $\alpha > \rho/R$.
- Section II: $X_t = (M_t, G_t)$; $G_t = (O_t, \Sigma_t)$; satisfaction-gap / control-regret split; orient cascade; directed separation as architectural classification (Class 1/2/3).
- Section III: composition closure, bridge lemma, sub-additive tempo, adversarial dynamics with effects spiral, equilibrium-convergence framing.
- Appendices: extensive — proofs, contraction-template generalization, LMI machinery, bias bound, identifiability-floor instances.

## What I expect to be most novel / consequential

- The persistence condition's substrate-independent form across Kalman / RL / orgs / software — substantively unifying.
- The identifiability-floor pattern with named escapes — it's a structural meta-claim, not just any individual no-go.
- The orient cascade as forced ordering — possibly the most under-recognized result in the corpus, given that LLM frameworks tend to fold this into a single attention pass.
- $G_t$ complexity bounded by $M_t$ capacity — a derived constraint that should rule out a lot of agentic-architecture moves people propose.
