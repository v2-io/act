# Reflection: def-adaptive-tempo

**Segment:** `#def-adaptive-tempo`

## What this does

Defines 𝒯 = Σ_k ν^(k) · η^(k)* — the product of loop speed and epistemic quality across all channels. Names the core capacity metric for the persistence condition.

Key insights from Discussion:
- Speed-quality substitutability: same tempo via fast noisy or slow calibrated loop
- Observation noise gating: can't outrun a bad observation channel by iterating faster
- Channel independence assumption: additive formula overcounts when channels are correlated (redundancy penalty)
- Scalar vs. vector tempo: scalar overcounts when correction is anisotropic

## Naming relevance

Row 104: "adaptive tempo" vs. alternatives.

- "Adaptive tempo" is the right name. "Tempo" carries both rate AND quality simultaneously — this is the card's exploration team's argument, and the segment confirms it: the product structure compounds "loop speed (ν) and epistemic quality (η*)." 
- "Adaptation rate" drops the quality dimension — it would name just ν, not the product.
- "Tempo" alone works as an informal alias but needs the adjective "adaptive" to distinguish from the raw event rate ν.
- Boyd/OODA lineage is confirmed: the Discussion explicitly says "grounds Boyd's emphasis on Orient quality over raw OODA speed." The Boyd connection is live.

## New concepts from this segment

**Redundancy penalty**: "effective correction capacity lost to overlapping information" when channels are correlated. Not sure if this is in the tracker.

**Strategic tempo (𝒯_Σ)**: Referenced as the strategy-edge revision analog. Row 136 or similar in tracker — this is the extension to Σ_t.

**Channel independence assumption**: Load-bearing limitation of the additive formula. Whether this is named in the tracker...

**Scalar vs. vector tempo**: A correction-isotropy assumption is hidden in the scalar form. The per-dimension persistence result is a separate named result.

## What's excellent here

The "observation noise gating" point is the most important corrective to naive thinking about tempo. Faster loops don't help if U_o is high. This is exactly what makes Boyd's critique of raw OODA speed correct — it's not just loop speed, it's the quality of the Orient phase.

The channel independence assumption and redundancy penalty are honest about a real limitation. The additive formula is an upper bound under correlation.
