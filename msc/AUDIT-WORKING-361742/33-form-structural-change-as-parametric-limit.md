# Reflection: form-structural-change-as-parametric-limit

**Segment:** `#form-structural-change-as-parametric-limit`

## What this does

Dissolves the sharp line between parametric updates (adjusting edge credences) and structural changes (adding/removing edges/nodes). In a probabilistic DAG, all six operations are continuous in the probability space:

1. Reweighting: p_ij changes (most frequent)
2. γ reclassification: AND↔OR combination type
3. Pruning: p_ij → ≈0 (remove failing branch)
4. Grafting: 0 → p_ij (add new causal hypothesis)
5. Objective revision: change terminal nodes
6. Full restructure: replace entire Σ_t (rarest, most expensive)

The Miller (2022) connection via extreme transition motif: neutral mutations = edges at p_ij ≈ 0 (latent structure, minimal cost); extreme transition = the moment when a previously near-zero-credence edge becomes load-bearing under regime change. An agent that prunes ALL low-credence edges loses latent diversity and becomes brittle.

Cross-agent grafting (symbiogenic composition) is when the grafted structure AND objective originate from another agent — bilateral vs. unilateral within-agent grafting.

## Naming relevance

**Row 167 (structural change as parametric limit)**: The naming question: is the five-word slug load-bearing?

- "Structural parametric limit": shorter but loses "change" which the defense correctly notes is load-bearing — the segment is about CHANGES to the DAG, not about DAG states.
- "Strategy maintenance": names the practical APPLICATION (the six operations are maintenance activities) but loses the theoretical CLAIM (that structural change is a parametric limit). The theoretical claim is the formulation's contribution.
- "Structural as parametric limit": close — dropping "change" is a mild compression, but "structural" without "change" is ambiguous (does it modify "parametric limit"? does it mean "structural-type"?).
- "structural change as parametric limit" (keep): long but accurate. The LENGTH is the cost; the PRECISION is the gain. For a formulation segment whose theoretical claim is this specific, precision over compactness.

My vote: keep +2 (the precision matters; this is a formulation, not a colloquial name).

But also worth considering: a shorter formulation name that doesn't lose the claim. Write-in: "DAG structural continuity" — names WHAT is established (that structural changes to the DAG are continuous). This might be cleaner.

## Key architectural insight confirmed

The segment confirms the pruning/grafting vocabulary (already voted +2 for "grafting" in prior sessions). The six-operation taxonomy is clean and useful. "Strategic hygiene" (mentioned in Discussion) is a good prose shorthand for the operation family but shouldn't be formalized as a term.

## Latent structure architecture

The Miller-bridge observation is the most architecturally important content: near-zero-credence edges are LATENT STRUCTURE. They represent alternative causal hypotheses that consume minimal cognitive cost (form-strategy-complexity-cost) but preserve exploratory capacity. Pruning for efficiency creates brittleness.
