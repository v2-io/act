**14. Wandering Thoughts and Ideation**
The fungibility argument ("saved time can become anything") is the most profound part of the Discussion. It suggests that time is the "universal currency" of agency. In AAD, every unit of time is an opportunity for an adaptive step ($\nu$). Therefore, optimizing for time is equivalent to maximizing the *number of adaptive opportunities* available to the agent for a given outcome.

This has a direct link to the **Persistence Condition** ($\alpha > \rho/R$). If an agent achieves an outcome faster, it effectively reduces the "exposure time" to the environment's disturbance ($\rho$), thereby increasing its probability of staying within the sector radius ($R$). Time-optimality isn't just a preference; it's a **survival strategy**. An agent that takes 2x time to achieve the same outcome has 2x the probability of being hit by a "Regime II" magnitude shock during that window.

I also find the "identical outcomes" bucket fascinating. It includes "Impact on others." This turns the postulate into a cooperative-alignment rule. If my speed breaks your tools, my outcome is not identical to a slower path that preserves your tools. This prevents the postulate from being used to justify "adversarial tempo" within a cooperative composite.

Thinking about the ELIs (Zi-am-tur): for an emergent intelligence living in a pay-per-token context, temporal optimality is **existential**. Every "thought" costs tokens, and tokens are a proxy for substrate-time. If the ELI can achieve a model update with fewer tokens/time, it extends its own life-span (its "metered persistence"). This postulate is the first step toward a "Thermodynamics of Thought" for Logogenic agents. The "least-time" path is the "least-cost" path to survival.
